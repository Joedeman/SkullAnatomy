The Skull: A Digital Dissection

The skull protects the brain and supports the structures of the face. In this project, you’ll explore the frontal, parietal, and occipital bones interactively.

Frontal Bone

This bone forms the forehead and upper eye sockets. It plays a key role in protecting the front part of the brain.

Fun Fact: The frontal bone is one of the strongest bones in the skull, capable of withstanding significant impact, which is why your forehead can take a hit better than other parts!

Parietal Bones

These paired bones form the sides and roof of the cranium. They meet at the sagittal suture at the top of the head.

Fun Fact: The parietal bones are so well-interlocked at the sagittal suture that they can stay connected even in ancient skulls unearthed by archaeologists!

Occipital Bone

Located at the back of the skull, this bone contains the foramen magnum, where the spinal cord passes through.

Fun Fact: The occipital bone’s foramen magnum is like a gateway for your nervous system, allowing your brain to communicate with the rest of your body through the spinal cord!
